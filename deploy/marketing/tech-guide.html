First Things First

Build a profile that gets you found.

Why this matters

Clients browse MNC on a map. If you don't have an address, you are literally invisible on it — like a shop with no front door. A complete profile with a real photo also makes clients three times more likely to reach out.

1

Sign in and open your Dashboard

Tap the person icon in the top right corner of the app. That's your home base — everything lives here.

2

Add your profile photo

Upload a clear photo of yourself or your salon's logo. Think of this as your first impression — clients decide within seconds whether they feel comfortable reaching out. Make it warm, bright, and you.

Good lighting, clear face or brand. No filters needed — real and professional wins every time.
3

Enter your full shop address

This is how you get pinned on the map. Be precise — include suite numbers if you work in a salon. If you're mobile-only, enter your general service area. No address = no map pin = clients can't find you nearby.

Mobile techs: tag yourself as Mobile in your profile and your street address is automatically hidden from public view. Clients see your service area and can still call or text — but your home base stays private. You can toggle this any time from the Dashboard under Address privacy.
4

Write your bio

A few sentences is perfect. Tell clients: how long you've been doing nails, what styles you love, and what makes your work different. Don't overthink it — write like you're texting a potential client. Something like: "10 years doing nails in Phoenix, specialty in nail art and gel extensions. My clients always feel like they left with something special."

5

Add your phone number

Clients can call or text directly from your profile. No messaging platform, no app — just your number. Add it so they have a way to reach you even if they don't want to book online.

Each phone number can only be used by one MNC account. If you get a "phone already in use" error at signup, it usually means you (or a previous account tied to that number) are already on MNC — use Sign in or reset your password rather than creating a duplicate.
6

Tap Save — you're on the map

Once saved, your pin appears on the map for clients in your area. You're officially visible. Now let's get your portfolio working for you.

03
The Most Important Step

Tag every photo. Every single one.

An untagged photo is invisible to most clients.

When clients use the browse filters — shape, material, style — only tagged photos show up. This is how the majority of clients browse. Skipping tags means your work never gets seen by the people most likely to book you.

Here's the deal: clients don't search for you specifically — they search for the look they want. "I want almond shape, gel, with flowers." MNC matches that search to photos with those tags. If your gorgeous floral set isn't tagged, it simply won't show up. Think of tags as the keywords that put your work in front of the right eyes.

Shape

Square Round Oval Almond Coffin Stiletto Ballerina Flare Squoval Lipstick Edge Arrowhead

Material

Gel Acrylic Gel-X Dip Powder Hard Gel BIAB Polygel Natural Press-On Gel Polish Shellac

Style

Nail Art French Ombre Glitter Solid Marble Floral Abstract Chrome Minimalist 3D Gems/Crystals Animal Print Holiday Geometric Baby Boomer

You can — and should — apply multiple style tags to one photo. That floral set? Tag it: Floral + 3D + Nail Art. The more accurate tags, the more clients find it.

1

Upload your photo

Tap "Add Photo" from your dashboard and choose the image from your camera roll.

2

Select the shape

Look at the nail in the photo and pick the closest match. If it's somewhere between square and oval, go with squoval. Don't overthink it — just be accurate.

3

Select the material

What product is on the nail? Gel, acrylic, dip? You know your work — pick the right one. Clients filter by this when they need a specific service.

4

Select all the styles that apply

This is where you can tag multiple options. A photo can be Chrome + Ombre + Abstract all at once. The more accurate, the better. Clients searching for any of those terms will see your photo.

5

Save the photo

It's now live on your profile and searchable in the browse feed. Every photo with proper tags is working for you around the clock, getting shown to clients who want exactly what you do.

The golden rule:

If you can't answer "what shape, what material, what style?" for a photo — the client can't find it either. Always tag before you save.

Already uploaded? Use the Tag My Photos dashboard.

If you had photos on your profile before tagging existed — or you skipped tags in a rush — head to Dashboard → Tag My Photos. Untagged photos appear full-size at the top with grouped tag chips (Shape / Material / Style); already-tagged photos collapse into a Tagged (N) drawer below. Tap chips and your changes auto-save about a second after the last tap. You can also tap any tagged thumbnail to re-edit. It's the fastest way to catch your whole portfolio up.

04
Get Them Booked

Connect your booking link — one tap to book you.

Why this matters

MNC doesn't handle bookings — it's how clients find you and get connected. Once a client falls in love with your work, they should be able to book you in one tap. If you're already on a booking platform, just paste your link. Done.

Paste your booking page link from any of these platforms and MNC will automatically label the button on your profile:

Vagaro Shows "Book on Vagaro"
StyleSeat Shows "Book on StyleSeat"
Square / GlossGenius / Other Shows "Book Now"
1

Grab your booking page link

Open your booking platform, find your public-facing booking page, and copy the URL. It's usually something like vagaro.com/yourname or square.site/yourname.

2

Paste it in your Dashboard under "Booking Link"

Look for the Booking Link field in your profile edit section. Just paste and save.

3

Preview how it looks on your profile

There's a preview button in the dashboard that shows how clients see your booking button. Give it a tap to make sure it points where you expect.

No booking platform? No problem. Clients can still call or text you directly using your phone number. Add both for maximum options.
05
Get Found First

Flip your availability. Jump to the front.

Why this is a secret weapon

Clients searching for a same-day appointment are ready to book right now. When you flip the availability toggle, you appear first in those searches. This is completely free and takes two seconds.

Available Right Now

Show up first for clients searching for same-day appointments

Available This Week

Appear in searches for clients booking within the next 7 days

1

Open your Dashboard any time you have openings

You don't have to keep this on all the time. Just flip it on when you have open appointments — first thing in the morning, when a client cancels, whenever.

2

Toggle "Available Right Now" or "Available This Week"

Use Right Now when you have same-day openings. Use This Week if you have spots in the next few days. You can turn both on if it applies.

3

Turn it off when you're booked up

There's no penalty for turning it on and off. The toggle is yours — use it like a "Open / Closed" sign in your window. Keep it honest so clients aren't disappointed when they reach out.

06
Stay Top of Mind

Post updates. Keep clients coming back.

Why posts work

Clients who already like your work check back on your profile. Posts show up right there — like a mini feed that's all yours. It keeps existing clients warm and gives new clients a reason to follow you.

The board is one-way — by design.

Posts on MNC are a broadcast, not a conversation. There are no replies, no comments, no threads. Clients read your update and then reach out the way they already know how — calling or texting your number, or tapping your booking link. That keeps the board clean, keeps you out of a DM inbox, and keeps every interaction pointing at a real booking.

You don't need to post every day. Just use it when you have something worth sharing. One post per hour, so pick your moment:

Availability windows

"I have 3 openings this Saturday — DM me to snag one!"

New nail inspo

"Just finished this set — obsessed with how the marble turned out."

Promos and specials

"New client special this month — 20% off your first set."

Fresh set photos

Drop a new set photo mid-week to keep your profile active and fresh.

1

Tap "New Post" from your Dashboard

It's right there on your dashboard home. One tap to open the composer.

2

Write your update and hit Post

Keep it short and real. You don't need to be a copywriter — just talk to your clients like you would in a text. It goes live on your profile instantly.
